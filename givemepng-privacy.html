GiveMePNG – Privacy Policy

This page explains how the GiveMePNG extension handles your data.

← Back to all privacy policies

Data Collection

The GiveMePNG extension does not collect any personal data. All processing of images happens locally within your browser.

External Services

The extension does not make any calls to external APIs or services. Your images are never sent to a server for processing.

Metadata

By default, the extension strips all EXIF metadata from images during the conversion process. This helps prevent sharing sensitive information like GPS location or camera details.

You can choose to preserve metadata via the extension's settings, but this is an opt‑in feature.

Storage

The extension uses chrome.storage to save your settings and the conversion counter. This data is stored locally in your browser and is not transmitted elsewhere.

The extension also includes a conservative, built-in blocklist of certain sensitive domains (for example, some banking, government, identity, and security-related sites). On these sites, GiveMePNG disables its hover-based image tools as an extra safety measure. This blocklist is implemented locally and does not involve any external lookups.

Future Features (Transparency)

We may introduce optional features later such as a global, anonymous conversion counter for social proof. If we do:

  • It will be strictly opt‑in and disabled by default
  • Only aggregate counts will be transmitted (no image data, no identifiers)
  • The endpoint domain will be explicitly listed in permissions
  • The privacy policy and store listing will be updated
